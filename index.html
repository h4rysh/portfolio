01 — ABOUT

Not another student portfolio. A point of view.

I care about the moment a messy problem becomes obvious, usable and hard to ignore. That is the standard I am building toward.

I do not want to ship noise. I want to build useful things with a pulse—products that make a decision, solve a real problem, and earn their space on someone’s screen.

My work sits between frontend craft, product thinking and relentless iteration. I like reducing friction, making information feel inevitable, and finding the one detail that turns an interface from fine into memorable.

I also occasionally edit video — mostly because rhythm has made me a better interface designer.

05 — HOW I WORK

Fast learner. Fussy finisher.

I am most useful when a problem needs both a clear point of view and the willingness to actually make the thing.

01 / BUILD

From blank canvas to working interface

I prototype quickly, then put the time into the states, spacing and edge cases that make a product feel considered.

02 / QUESTION

Ask what the screen is really for

I prefer one strong reason for every element over a page full of decoration pretending to be strategy.

03 / REFINE

Keep going after “good enough”

The last pass is where hierarchy gets sharper, motion gets quieter and the experience starts to feel like it belongs.

07 — THE STANDARD

Three things I refuse to compromise on.

A compact operating system for the kind of work I want to be known for.

01

Make it clear.

If a user has to pause and decode the interface, the design has not done its job yet.

02

Make it count.

Every feature, word and animation should earn its existence by moving the product forward.

03

Make it memorable.

Utility is the baseline. A distinct point of view is what gives useful work a longer life.
